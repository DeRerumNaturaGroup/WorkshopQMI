Different Methods of Simulations

DFT - A Workhorse of Computational Materials Science

DFT provides a practical balance between accuracy and computational cost, making quantum-mechanical simulations possible for real materials. Wavefunction methods try to track every interaction, while DFT focuses on the electron density, making the problem much more manageable.

✔ Quantum mechanical description of materials

✔ Reasonably accurate for many properties

✔ Applicable to solids, molecules, surfaces, and nanomaterials

✔ Scales to hundreds of atoms

✔ Large ecosystem of software and databases

✔ Can predict properties before experiments e..g,

  • energy of the system
  • dielectric coefficient
  • adsorption spectra
  • adsorption energy
  • phonons
  • defect concentration
  • defect stability etc

WHAT IS DFT?

DFT solves Many Body Problem

But How?

By Solving Schrodinger equation - a fundamental equation governing the behavior and evolution of quantum particles:

\[ \hat{H}\psi = E\psi \qquad(1)\]

Hamiltonian for complex systems:

\[ \hat{H} = -\frac{1}{2} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \nabla_i^2 - \frac{1}{2} \sum_{A=1}^{M} \frac{1}{M_A} \nabla_A^2 - \sum_{i=1}^{N}\sum_{A=1}^{M} \frac{Z_A}{r_{iA}} + \sum_{i=1}^{N}\sum_{j>1}^{N} \frac{1}{r_{ij}} + \sum_{A=1}^{M}\sum_{B>A}^{M} \frac{Z_AZ_B}{r_{AB}} \qquad(2)\]

Is it practical to consider each electron in a system?

Density Functional Theory (DFT) uses the electron density as the central quantity to determine and predict all physical and chemical properties of a material.

WHAT IS DFT?

① How to estimate interactions?

\[ \text{Total interactions} = \text{known} + \text{unknown} \]

Hence Exchange Correlation Functionals

② Do all electrons matter equally?

\[ \text{Total electron density} = \text{core} + \text{valence} \]

Hence Pseudopotentials

Glossary

Many-Body Problem

The quantum mechanical problem of describing a system containing many interacting electrons and nuclei. Since every electron interacts with every other electron through Coulomb forces, solving the Schrödinger equation exactly becomes computationally intractable for all but the smallest systems.

Exchange–Correlation (XC) Functional

A mathematical approximation that accounts for the quantum mechanical exchange and electron correlation effects not described exactly in the Kohn–Sham equations. The choice of XC functional largely determines the accuracy of a DFT calculation.

Correlation Energy

The energy associated with the correlated motion of electrons due to their mutual Coulomb repulsion, beyond the mean-field approximation.

Electron Density

The probability density of finding electrons at different positions in space. In Density Functional Theory, the electron density replaces the many-electron wavefunction as the central quantity used to determine the properties of a system.

Exchange Energy

The contribution to the total energy arising from the antisymmetric nature of the electronic wavefunction required by the Pauli exclusion principle.

Pseudopotential

An effective potential that replaces the nucleus and tightly bound core electrons, allowing only the chemically active valence electrons to be treated explicitly. This significantly reduces computational cost while maintaining good accuracy.

DFT Steps

Step 1 - Convergence

  • Energy Cut-off
  • k-points

Step 2 - Structure Relaxation

  • Atomic positions
  • Both atomic positions and lattice

Step 3 - Self Consistent Field Calculations

An SCF calculation is performed to determine the electronic ground state of a material for a given atomic structure. During the SCF process, the electron density is updated iteratively until a self-consistent solution is achieved, where the input and output electron densities become nearly identical. The converged SCF calculation provides accurate total energy, charge density, and other electronic properties that serve as the basis for further analysis.

Step 4 - Calculation of Physical Properties

The final DFT electronic structure serves as the foundation for calculating various material properties, including band structures, density of states, optical absorption spectra, dielectric response, thermoelectric transport coefficients, adsorption energies, and catalytic reaction energetics.

Band Structure Calculation

It is a 2 step process.

  1. non-self-consistent field (NSCF) calculation along a chosen high-symmetry k-point path using the fixed charge density from the SCF step.

  2. A post-processing step using bands.x is used to organize and extract the eigenvalues to generate the final band structure plot.

Steps to Follow:

  1. Open the QECalcs folder.
  2. Open the ep1_bands folder.
  3. Open the bands.in file.
  4. Run the calculation on the terminal:
mpirun -np 2 pw.x -i bands.in > bands.out

  1. Bands Post Processing:

    • In the same folder, open the file bandspp.in.
    • Run the command in the terminal:
mpirun -np 2 bands.x -i bandspp.in > bandspp.out

Non Self Consistent (NSCF) Calculation

An NSCF calculation is performed using a fixed charge density obtained from a previously converged SCF calculation to calculate the eigen values and wavefunctions at a chosen set of dense k-points without updating the charge density to perform post-processing tasks such as density of states (DOS) or band structure calculations.

Steps to Follow:

  1. Open the QECalcs folder.
  2. Open the ep2_nscf folder.
  3. Open the nscf.in file.
  4. Run the calculation on the terminal:
mpirun -np 2 pw.x -i nscf.in > nscf.out

The Computational Scientist’s Mindset

Don’t be afraid of the terminal

“The terminal doesn’t know you’re a beginner—it simply follows your instructions.”

Errors are normal

Every computational scientist sees errors every single day.

An error is not a failure.

It is simply the computer telling you what it didn’t understand.

Understand what you paste

Instead of memorizing commands, understand what they do, know why you’re running them. This is how everyone learns.

Don’t try to memorize commands

Instead, learn the workflow.

Ask questions

If something isn’t clear,

someone else is probably wondering the same thing.

Questions help everyone learn.

Google (and ChatGPT) are part of the workflow

Nobody remembers every command.

Professional researchers constantly search

Linux commands Python syntax Quantum ESPRESSO errors Slurm errors

The important skill isn’t memorizing everything—it’s knowing how to find reliable information.

Read the error before asking for help

Many beginners immediately say

“It doesn’t work.”

Instead, teach them to say

“This command produced this error.”

Often the last few lines of an error message tell you exactly what’s wrong.

Installation is usually the hardest part

Running calculations is often easier than setting up the software.

So don’t become discouraged if installation takes time.

We’ve all been there.

Keep experimenting

You won’t break Quantum ESPRESSO by changing

k-points cutoff energy smearing

That’s how computational research is done.
